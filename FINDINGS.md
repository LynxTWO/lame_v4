# LAME v4 Findings and Results

Written for two readers at once. A normal reader should understand what changed, why it
matters, and why the results can be trusted. An engineer should be able to reproduce every
number, challenge every assumption, and extend the work without guessing.

> MP3 is a fixed 25-year-old format, but how well an encoder uses it still has headroom.
>
> We built the measurement floor first. Then we used it to find and fix a real quality
> regression in LAME 3.100, fix the quantizer's search objective, add bit-exact
> multithreading, and run machine-tuning campaigns with holdout discipline. Every claim
> below carries its receipt, including the dead ends.

---

## 1. What this project is

The goal is simple. Make LAME encode better MP3s, at reasonable cost.

Quality comes first. Speed is welcome, but never at quality's expense. Modern CPUs have
16 to 32 cores sitting idle during an encode, so the encoder can afford to think harder.

Encoding is an optimization problem. Given a bit budget, LAME decides where to place the
rounding damage. Good encoding hides that damage under sounds the ear already hears. Bad
encoding spends bits in the wrong places and leaves audible scars.

The rule for this project is blunt: no claim survives without a receipt. We do not say
something sounds better because the code looks clever. We measure it on hard material,
against a baseline, and keep the change only when the evidence holds.

---

## 2. Measurement foundation

The tools came before any encoder change, because codec work is full of traps. A change can
sound different but measure worse. A gate can look green because the feature never ran. A
search can optimize the metric it is judged by while making the decoded audio worse. All
three of those happened in this project, and the tools caught them.

### 2.1 Bit-exact regression gate

Plain English: a tripwire. Some changes must not alter the MP3 output at all: refactors,
build-system changes, threading parity, inactive features. If one byte changes, the gate
trips.

Engineer receipt: `tests/regress.ps1` encodes `tests/regress_corpus` (a deterministic
synthetic set regenerated by `tests/corpusgen`) at V0, V2, V5, CBR 320, CBR 128, `-q0`
CBR 320, and ABR 192, then stores SHA-256 hashes of both the MP3 bytes and the decoded PCM.
The 70-case baseline lives in `tests/baseline.json`.

```powershell
pwsh tests/regress.ps1                            # compare; nonzero exit on any drift
pwsh tests/regress.ps1 -ExtraArgs '--threads 2'   # same baseline, feature enabled
```

The `-ExtraArgs` form lets bit-identical-by-design options prove themselves against the
same baseline.

### 2.2 Independent perceptual meter

Plain English: the tool that answers the real question. How much of the encoding error
rises above what the ear can mask? Lower is better.

The key safeguard is independence. The meter does not reuse LAME's psychoacoustic model. It
compares original audio against decoded MP3 output with its own masking model, so the
encoder cannot grade its own homework.

Engineer receipt: `tests/nmr` computes a decoded-domain noise-to-mask ratio per critical
band on a Bark scale. It derives a masking threshold from the original (band energy, a
spreading function, absolute threshold in quiet), measures original-vs-decoded error
energy, compensates for encoder and decoder delay by cross-correlation, and reports
`meanNMRdb` (the primary number), audible fraction, audible mean, and max.

The meter earned trust through validation. What to look for: more bits should measure
cleaner, and identical audio should measure transparent.

| Check | Result |
| --- | --- |
| Bitrate monotonicity (music) | 128 kbps ~ -3.5 dB, 320 kbps ~ -15.3 dB |
| Self-identity | any file vs itself ~ -100 dB, zero audible bands |
| Cross-platform determinism | -59.0308 reproduced to four decimals on Windows and Linux |

One honest limit, discovered by the CI smoke test: mostly-silent files bottom out at the
meter's epsilon-vs-tiny-mask floor (~ -59 dB even for self-comparison), so identity checks
must use dense material. An earlier version of this section claimed synthetic torture
signals were not bitrate-monotone; that was wrong, and the mechanism is in the pitfalls
table below. With correctly tag-aligned decodes, the synthetics are strongly monotone too
(music_mix -3.49 at 128 vs -15.25 at 320; pink noise -2.40 vs -14.74).

### 2.3 A/B harness

Plain English: the scoreboard. It encodes every corpus file with two encoders or two
settings, scores both with the meter, and reports who won and by how much.

```powershell
pwsh tests/abtest.ps1 -A base\lame.exe -B variant\lame.exe -Setting '-q 0 -b 128'
```

Use `-SettingB` to compare two settings instead of two builds. Negative delta means B is
more transparent at equal bitrate. The A-equals-B self-check reads 0.000 across the corpus,
which confirms determinism.

`tests/validate_qmax.ps1` is the four-metric variant: it captures mean, audible fraction,
audible mean, and worst band per file, which is how metric-gaming gets caught.
`tests/transient` measures pre-echo and transient high-frequency fidelity at detected
attacks.

### 2.4 Test material

A codec that only wins on friendly samples has not won. Four classes of material, kept in
strict roles:

| Corpus | Role |
| --- | --- |
| `tests/regress_corpus/` | 10 deterministic synthetic torture signals; bit-exact gate only |
| `tests/corpus/` | synthetics plus real music, including Tom's Diner |
| `tests/corpus/SQAM/` | EBU SQAM, 70 professional critical recordings; holdout |
| `tests/corpus_train / _val / _holdout` | library excerpts for tuning campaigns, with provenance manifests; train is searched, val vetoes, holdout judges |

Corpus audio is never committed. It is regenerable, user-supplied, or copyrighted. The
generators, manifests, and baseline hashes are what make results reproducible.

### 2.5 Measurement pitfalls

Each of these caused a wrong conclusion in this project before being caught. Check for them
in any ad-hoc benchmark.

| Trap | Failure mode | Rule now |
| --- | --- | --- |
| PowerShell automatic variables | `$a`/`$A` alias each other; `$args` is reserved, so assigning encode settings to it makes the splat expand to nothing and every "variant" runs at defaults | use distinct names (`$encArgs`, `$mA`, `$mB`) |
| CPU contention | timing runs taken while corpus validations run in the background are garbage | time on an idle machine; sanity-check totals against stage instrumentation |
| Trivially green gates | a parity pass only proves something if the feature actually engaged; dead code passes everything | pair every gate with a measurement that shows the code path ran |
| Missing info tag (`-t`) before the meter | without the LAME tag the decoder cannot strip encoder delay/padding; decoded audio is misaligned and the meter reads ~+14 dB of pure offset artifact with audible fraction ~1.0 | encodes that feed `nmr` must keep the tag; `-t` stays correct only in the hash-comparing bit-exact gate |

The `$args` trap corrupted an entire benchmark round. It briefly convinced us that
`--quality-max` was cost-neutral and that threading was useless. The missing-tag trap was
worse: it silently degraded the fitness signal of auto-tuning campaigns 1 through 6, blinded
their audibility constraints, and produced the false "synthetics are not monotone" claim.
It was caught when the campaign-6 veto passed a config whose holdout audNMR was known bad,
and a direct per-file measurement disagreed with the tool. All corrections are recorded in
the findings below.

## 3. Findings

### Finding 1. LAME 3.100's `-q0` was worse than `-q4` at CBR and ABR. Confirmed and fixed.

LAME's quality dial runs from `-q9` (fast, rough) to `-q0` (slow, best). At fixed bitrate,
`-q0` should never lose to the middle setting.

It did. At CBR 128, stock `-q0` measured **+0.333 dB worse** than stock `-q4` on average,
and worse still on real music: 400 Lux +0.44, Tom's Diner +0.40, sample20 +1.37 dB.

An external tip claimed `-q0` through `-q3` can regress against `-q4` at CBR and ABR. We
tested it instead of arguing with it (`abtest.ps1` with `-SettingB`). The claim held.

#### Root cause

At `-q0`, `libmp3lame/lame.c` sets a VBR-tuned noise-shaping configuration:
`noise_shaping_amp = 2` (amplify only the single worst scalefactor band per `outer_loop`
iteration) and `substep_shaping = 2` (pseudohalf half-stepping). Both assume the outer loop
iterates freely to convergence, which is true in VBR. Under a fixed CBR or ABR bit budget
the loop terminates early, out of bits, so the fine-grained shaping never finishes
distributing the noise. Stock `-q4`'s broad amplify-all-bands approach spreads it better.

#### Fix

For bit-constrained modes only (`cfg->vbr == vbr_off || vbr_abr`), route `-q0` through the
previously unused `noise_shaping_amp = 3` path: a coarse pass (amp 1) followed by a fine
refinement pass (amp 2), with `full_outer_loop = 0` and substep shaping left off. True VBR
keeps its existing behavior. Original branch `qa/fix-noiseshaping-cbr`, commit `0e346c9`.

#### Results

Fixed `-q0` vs stock `-q0`, meanNMRdb. Lower is better; negative means improvement.

| Material | Setting | Files better | Corpus mean |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Music + synthetic (32 files) | CBR 128 | 22 / 32 | ~ -0.35 |
| Music + synthetic | CBR 320 | - | -0.78 |
| Music + synthetic | ABR 192 | - | -0.49 |
| EBU SQAM (70 files) | CBR 128 | 59 / 70 | -0.926 |
| EBU SQAM | CBR 320 | 59 / 70 | -1.576 |
| EBU SQAM | ABR 192 | 58 / 70 | -0.608 |
| VBR (V0, V2) | - | - | 0.000 (untouched) |

Worst SQAM regression: +0.10 dB. Biggest single gain: -15.4 dB on a sample where stock
`-q0` badly misallocated. The bit-exact gate showed byte drift in exactly the 10
`-q0`-CBR test cases and nothing else, so the change is surgically confined to its target.

Human ABX (2026-07-02): the project owner scored **15/16, p = 0.0003** in foobar2000 v2.25
(foo_abx 2.2.3) on the Tom's Diner clip, stock vs fixed `-q0` at CBR 128. ABX proves
audible difference, not preference. On that excerpt the fixed encode measures -0.55 dB more
transparent (-6.03 vs -5.48) with fewer audible bands (0.261 vs 0.294), so the objective
evidence identifies the fixed encode as the cleaner one.

#### Status

Merged to the default `-q0` at `e592af3` and re-baselined. Validated four ways: perceptual
meter, SQAM, byte-isolation, human ABX.

### Finding 2. `--quality-max`: an opt-in maximum-effort mode.

A new switch that tells LAME to work much harder for quality. It never changes any existing
setting; it only does more when asked.

Engineer receipt: new flag `--quality-max` (alias `--qmax`), API `lame_set_quality_max`,
config `cfg->quality_max`. Version 1 built on the `-q0` configuration with the in-loop
Huffman search restored (the search `-q0` disables for speed; see Finding 0) and the
Finding 1 CBR/ABR shaping.

Measured against stock `-q0`, meanNMRdb, lower is better:

| Mode | v1 vs stock `-q0` |
| --- | --- |
| CBR 128 | -0.357 |
| CBR 320 | -0.753 |
| ABR 192 | -0.605 |
| V0 | 0.000 by equal-setting NMR |

The VBR row needs a caveat: the Huffman search helps VBR as smaller files at equal quality,
which an equal-setting NMR comparison cannot see.

Safety receipt: purely additive, bit-exact gate 70/70 green for every existing setting.

Build warning that bit this project: `Makefile.MSVC` does not track header dependencies.
After editing a widely included header (`util.h`, `lame_global_flags.h`), run
`build.cmd clean && build.cmd`, or stale objects with mismatched struct layouts encode
successfully and then crash on exit.

#### Status

Merged. Superseded in strength by v2 (Finding 3), which is the current behavior of the flag.

### Finding 3. The quantizer optimized the wrong objective. `--quality-max` v2 fixed it.

For 25 years the high-quality comparison rule has been: make the single worst band less
bad. Under a fixed bit budget that rule plays whack-a-mole with damage instead of reducing
total audible damage.

Switching the objective was worth more than the entire Finding 1 fix: **-0.944 dB at
CBR 128, 33 of 33 files better, zero regressions.**

#### The dead ends that pointed at it

Two reasonable ideas failed first, and the failures located the real problem.

Protecting high-frequency bands on transients (tightening their allowed distortion in
`calc_xmin`): crisper on only 2 of 6 transient tracks, and worse overall at both 128 and
320 kbps. Rejected and reverted. At a fixed budget, demanding more precision in one place
steals it from everywhere else.

Searching harder on the stock objective (`full_outer_loop = 1`): worse again, +0.15 dB at
CBR 128 and +0.02 at CBR 320. That was the tell. If searching harder makes the result
worse, the search is optimizing the wrong thing, and the early-stop had been acting as
accidental damage control.

#### The objective sweep

`outer_loop` compares candidate quantizations with `quant_compare` (`quantize.c`), selected
by `cfg->quant_comp`. Mode 1 minimizes `max_noise`, the single worst band. Mode 2 minimizes
`tot_noise`, the sum of per-band log noise-to-mask. Corpus mean meanNMRdb at CBR 128, same
binary, lower is better:

| Objective (long blocks) | Normal depth | Exhaustive (`full_outer_loop=1`) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `max_noise` (stock q0) | -7.545 (baseline) | -7.392 (worse) |
| `tot_noise` (mode 2) | -8.332 | -8.460 (winner) |
| `klemm` (mode 8) | -8.087 | -8.096 |

Two things confirm this is not metric-gaming. The klemm objective, a cubic-penalty
aggregate structurally different from our meter, also wins. And the guardrails below moved
the right way.

#### Guardrails

Winner vs `--quality-max` v1 at CBR 128. What to look for: the win must show up in
audibility, not just the mean, and nothing may blow up.

| Guardrail | Result |
| --- | --- |
| Mean NMR | -0.944 dB, 33/33 files better, worst per-file delta +0.000 |
| Audible fraction | 0.319 -> 0.277 |
| Worst single band | 98.7 -> 98.7 (unchanged) |
| Transient HF | crisper on 5/6 tracks at CBR 128 (castanets 3.84 -> 2.91); 3 wins, 3 ties at CBR 320 |
| Bit-exact gate | 70/70 green for all non-`--quality-max` settings |

The audible-fraction drop matters most. Both `tot_noise` and the meter's mean give credit
for pushing already-inaudible bands further down; the drop in audible band-frames proves
the win is real audibility, not margin-stacking. The one transient "regression" (400 Lux)
is a single onset whose original HF sits near silence at -22 dB, and v2 has less pre-echo
there.

The short-block objective (`quant_comp_short`) was swept too (modes 0/2/8: -8.460 /
-8.467 / -8.444, a statistical tie). Stock mode 0 stays: it won the transient tiebreak
(Tom's Diner HF 0.59 vs 0.83), and bounding the count of distorted bands on short blocks
bounds transient smearing.

#### Results

Final `--quality-max` v2 vs v1, corpus mean. Lower is better.

| Mode | v2 vs v1 |
| --- | --- |
| CBR 128 | -0.944 dB |
| CBR 320 | -0.982 dB |
| ABR 192 | -0.761 dB |

Cost is the price of the win: ~15x default `-q0` (Tom's Diner CBR 128: 22.3 s vs 1.5 s;
CBR 320: 39.9 s; ABR 192: 29.8 s). Still faster than realtime single-threaded, and
`--threads 2` recovers up to a third of it (Finding 4).

Correction history, kept for trust: we briefly reported this mode as cost-neutral. That
benchmark had assigned its encode settings to PowerShell's reserved `$args` variable, so
every measurement ran at default settings (section 2.5). Stage instrumentation settled it:
the ~22 s figure was right.

#### Status

Merged (`c1034a2`). Scoped to CBR/ABR like Finding 1; the objective override lives in
`lame_init_params` after the `cfg->quant_comp` copy, because an earlier write gets
clobbered. VBR keeps stock objectives until an equal-size methodology exists. A
reproduction note: cross-build LTCG floating-point wobble makes absolute corpus means drift
by ~0.02 dB between binaries; all decisions here used same-binary deltas.

### Finding 4. `--threads 2`: bit-exact channel-parallel quantization.

Threading is useful only if it cannot change the file. This one cannot: output is
byte-for-byte identical to single-threaded, and the gate proves it on every push.

Measured on Tom's Diner, 1 thread vs 2:

| Setting | 1 thread | 2 threads | Speedup |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `--quality-max` CBR 320 | 39.9 s | 25.9 s | 1.54x |
| `--quality-max` ABR 192 | 30.2 s | 19.3 s | 1.56x (frame-batched) |
| `--quality-max` CBR 128 | 22.3 s | 19.0 s | 1.17x |
| default `-q0` CBR 128 | 1.5 s | 1.4 s | 1.06x |

#### Why it is bit-exact

The per-(granule, channel) quantization body was extracted verbatim into `quantize_gr_ch`
(the refactor alone re-verified 70/70 bit-exact). A persistent Win32 worker runs channel
1's search while the main thread runs channel 0's. The decomposition rests on an audited
shared-state inventory:

| State | Why it is safe |
| --- | --- |
| `sv_qnt.CurrentStep[ch]`, `OldValue[ch]` | disjoint per-channel slots; granules of one channel chain through them and stay sequential |
| `sv_qnt.masking_lower` | never read by the search (its only readers are in `psymodel.c`, next frame); writes stay on the main thread in loop order |
| `sv_qnt.pseudohalf[]` | only live under substep shaping; every access is gated on `substep_shaping & 2`, and the threaded mode requires substep off |
| search internals | `calc_xmin`, `outer_loop`, `count_bits` read `gfc` as const and write only `cod_info` and locals |

Eligibility is deliberately narrow: stereo, CBR or ABR, substep off. Everything else runs
the untouched sequential path. Verified: `tests/regress.ps1` passes all 70 cases with and
without `-ExtraArgs '--threads 2'` against the same baseline.

ABR dispatch is frame-batched: ABR knows every granule's bit target up front
(`calc_target_bits`), so each thread runs one channel's two granules in order, which
preserves the per-channel search chain, amortizes channel imbalance, and halves the joins
(1.50x -> 1.56x, outputs hash-identical). CBR cannot batch: granule 1's targets depend on
granule 0's reservoir accounting, so its 1.17x at 128 kbps is close to structural.

#### Where the time goes

Stage instrumentation on Tom's Diner explains why quantization was the right first target
and where any future speed work must aim:

| Setting | psymodel | MDCT | quantization | bitstream |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| default `-q0` CBR 128 | 26% | 13% | 58% | 3% |
| `--quality-max` CBR 128 | 2% | 1% | 97% | 0% |
| `-V 2` | 24% | 11% | 61% | 4% |

The analysis front (psymodel + MDCT) was audited separately
(`docs/analysis-front-audit.md`): channel-parallel is feasible bit-exactly but worth only
~1.2x at default settings, so it is deferred.

#### Status

Merged. Off by default; opt-in via `--threads 2` / `lame_set_threads`.

### Finding 5. Portfolio search: a measured dead end that relocated the frontier.

We tried to make `--quality-max` search even harder: run the complete quantization search
once under each of LAME's four shaping strategies per granule and channel, keep the best
finished candidate.

Every selection rule made real music worse. The lesson is not that deeper search is
useless. It is that **LAME's internal accounting cannot referee structurally different
candidates**, and no per-granule referee can.

#### What was built, and verified exact first

| Piece | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| `gr_info.qmax_shaping` | per-(granule, channel) strategy override |
| per-run state isolation | each run starts from the same `CurrentStep`/`OldValue`; the winning run's state carries forward |
| re-derivation from untouched `xr` | every run recomputes from the same spectrum, so scores are comparable |
| floor test | a portfolio restricted to strategy 3 is byte-identical to v2 across a full real track |

The floor test matters: the harness was exact, so the regressions below are real properties
of cross-strategy selection, not tool bugs.

#### Results

Four selection rules, each stricter, on a 4-file real-music subset at CBR 128. Positive
means regression vs v2.

| Selection rule | vs v2 | Failure mechanism |
| --- | --- | --- |
| fewest unmasked bands, then tot_noise | +2.32 dB | reintroduces the minimax pathology: worst-band strategies win `over_count` while wrecking aggregate noise |
| tot_noise only (the search's own objective) | +1.93 dB | locally greedy, globally destructive: cheap strategies spend the granule budget that v2 leaves for hard frames downstream (bit-reservoir drain) |
| full Pareto dominance (`over_count` AND `over_noise` AND `tot_noise` AND bits, at least one strict) | +0.45 dB | swaps every internal metric calls unambiguous upgrades still measure worse on the independent meter |
| dual referee: strictly better by an independent Bark-spread mask rebuilt in the MDCT domain, AND tot_noise no worse, AND bits no worse | +0.20 dB | even swaps endorsed by two masking models at no bit cost leak quality through channels invisible at selection time |

The convergence tells the story: +2.32 -> +1.93 -> +0.45 -> +0.20, monotone from below,
never crossing zero.

The last two leak channels are structural, not fixable by better scoring. A swap changes
the scalefactor structure, which changes cross-granule `scfsi` compression after selection;
the bit guard sees pre-finish sizes. And the meter measures decoded PCM whose analysis
windows span granule boundaries, invisible to any per-granule score.

#### What the failure taught

v2's trajectory is at or near the achievable optimum for this candidate class. Deeper
search research must go through end-to-end evaluation: change a global parameter, encode
completely, judge with the external meter. That architecture has no per-granule selection
to leak, and it is exactly what Finding 6 does.

#### Status

Rejected and compiled out. The whole apparatus (strategy override, state isolation,
second-referee mask and scorer) stays in the tree behind `-DLAME_QMAX_PORTFOLIO`, verified
byte-identical to v2 when compiled out; gates 70/70 with and without `--threads 2`. Cost
for reference: ~3x v2 (66 s vs 22 s on the ABX track at CBR 128), threads-identical.

### Finding 6. Meter-driven auto-tuning: the 2002 hand-tuned constants can be beaten, and the search fights back.

LAME's psychoacoustic model carries half a dozen tuning constants set by human listening
around 2002. It turns out the command-line knobs for them have been compile-locked off in
every normal build since: `--ns-bass`, `--ns-alto`, `--ns-treble`, `--ns-sfb21`,
`--nsmsfix`, and `--shortthreshold` parse and silently do nothing without
`_ALLOW_INTERNAL_OPTIONS`.

The dead-knob trap announced itself as a perfect result: the first campaign returned 177
configs with byte-identical output, "proving" stock optimal. The section 2.5 discipline
caught it. The v4 build now defines the flag (frontend-only; option-free encodes stay
bit-exact, gate-verified), which means this tuning surface had likely never been
systematically explored with an objective metric.

#### Method

`tools/autotune` runs end-to-end parameter search: each config encodes a complete train
corpus and is scored by the independent meter; configs run 32 at a time; the random phase
uses a deterministic seed; refinement rounds shrink around the incumbent. SQAM and the
holdout tracks never enter any search. Campaign corpora come from the owner's CD library as
30-second excerpts with provenance manifests (train), plus fresh library tracks reserved
for validation and holdout.

#### Campaign 1. Conservative bounds produced a validated candidate.

Search: 6 knobs (region masking adjust +/- 4 dB, `nsmsfix` 0.5 to 3.5, short-block
threshold factor 0.5x to 2x), 128 random configs plus 2 refinement rounds, at `-q0`
CBR 128.

Winner:

```text
--ns-bass 0.57 --ns-alto 2.37 --ns-treble 4.00 --ns-sfb21 2.89 --nsmsfix 0.63 --shortthreshold 5.92,33.63
```

It generalizes better than it trained, which is the opposite of the usual overfitting
smell. Lower is better:

| Set | vs stock `-q0` CBR 128 |
| --- | --- |
| train (13 files, what the search saw) | -0.055 dB |
| SQAM holdout (70 files, never seen) | -0.183 dB |
| full-length tracks holdout | -0.236 dB |
| fresh 16-track library holdout | -0.355 dB |

Transient check: sub-dB and split across four attack-heavy tracks (sample20 better on both
metrics), slight HF dulling on two tracks, the expected face of relaxed treble masking.

Status: **candidate, opt-in only, pending ABX** (the D-vs-E pair in `tests/abx/`). Not a
default change.

#### Campaign 2. Full bounds memorized the train set.

The winner rode its +/- 4 bound, so campaign 2 searched the full representable range
(+/- 8 dB, the `parse.c` clamp) with 8 knobs (adding the temporal-masking toggle and
`--athlower`; `--interch` measured dead under the 3.100 psymodel and was dropped).

Train said -0.212 dB with an extreme config (bass/alto/treble pinned at -8/+8/-8, treble
sign-flipped vs campaign 1, temporal masking off). Holdout said **+1.44 dB worse on SQAM
and +2.03 dB worse on full-length tracks.**

The mean got better on train because the search memorized it. The conservative bounds were
the regularization. Rejected. The overfitting boundary for this space and a 13-file train
set sits between +/- 4 and +/- 8.

#### Campaign 3. More data moved the mean and exposed the deeper problem.

The train set grew to 36 files (27 real-music excerpts). The campaign-1 winner passed its
third holdout here (-0.355 dB above). The full +/- 8 search now generalized on the mean:
-0.418 dB on SQAM, -1.052 dB on the library holdout.

The guardrails rejected it anyway. Transient HF error exploded on real music (Tom's Diner
0.67 -> 3.38; 400 Lux 1.98 -> 9.97: `ns-treble +8` lets the encoder trash treble at
attacks), and while the audible fraction dropped (0.32 -> 0.28), the errors that stayed
audible nearly doubled in loudness (audNMR 5.2 -> 9.0).

The mean got better because the errors became fewer and louder. That is not a win. That is
the metric getting played.

#### Campaigns 4 and 5. Train-side constraints do not transfer.

Campaign 4 penalized audNMR regression as a train-set mean. It never fired: the synthetic
torture files' huge audNMR drowns a music-only regression (Simpson's paradox inside the
fitness), and the winner was identical to campaign 3's.

Campaign 5 made the constraint per-file (no training file may exceed its own stock audNMR
by more than 0.1 dB, lambda 10). The search produced a different winner
(`--ns-bass -6.01 --ns-alto -7.86 --ns-treble 6.26 --ns-sfb21 -7.79 --nsmsfix 0.51
--shortthreshold 2.62,14.89 --athlower -5.59`) with even better holdout means (-0.612 SQAM,
-1.377 library) that satisfied the constraint on train and failed on holdout in the
identical way: music audNMR ~9.0, Tom's Diner transient HF 0.67 -> 6.06.

Two independent configs, same unseen-music pathology. At wide masking bounds, large mean
gains at CBR 128 appear inseparable from redistributing audibility into fewer-but-louder
errors, and train-side constraints relocate the exploit rather than remove it. Both
rejected.

#### Campaign 6. The validation-split veto that could not fail, and the bug it exposed.

The next escalation moved the transfer test inside the loop: a validation split of 12 fresh
library tracks that the fitness never optimizes, where a candidate must keep every file's
audNMR within +0.5 dB of stock (and the mean within +0.1) before it can become the
incumbent or the winner (`tools/autotune --val`).

The run passed every vet and crowned campaign 5's already-rejected winner. That was
impossible: a direct per-file measurement showed that config exceeding the cap on all 12
val tracks by +3.3 to +3.9 dB. The contradiction led to the missing-tag trap (section 2.5):
the tool's encodes carried `-t`, so every fitness and veto measurement since campaign 1 ran
on misaligned decodes. The internal audNMR baselines read ~14.8 where correctly aligned
values sit near 5, which is why the constraints of campaigns 4 and 5 had so little grip and
the veto saw nothing.

Scope of the damage, stated precisely: campaign fitness signals and in-loop constraints were
degraded; every holdout and guardrail number in this finding is unaffected, because
`abtest.ps1`, `validate_qmax.ps1`, and the transient meter never used `-t`. The fix removes
`-t` from the tool's measurement chain; campaign 7 reruns the search with a meaningful
fitness and a veto that can actually fire.

#### Status

Campaign-1 winner: candidate, pending ABX (found under the degraded fitness, but validated
entirely by correctly measured holdouts, so its receipts stand). Campaigns 2 through 5:
rejected on correctly measured holdouts and guardrails; receipts in
`tests/autotune_q0_cbr128.csv` through `autotune5_q0_cbr128.csv`. Campaign 6: superseded by
the bug it caught (`autotune6_q0_cbr128.csv`). Campaign 7 (fixed measurement chain):
pending.

### Finding 0 (minor). Re-enabling the in-loop Huffman search.

Stock `-q0` disables its own thorough Huffman sub-search purely for speed; its source
comment says so. Re-enabling it (`use_best_huffman = 2`, `takehiro.c:747`) costs every
candidate quantization with optimal Huffman partitioning, so the search makes better
bit-budget decisions.

Measured at `-q0 -b128` on the early corpus: -0.030 dB mean, 6 of 10 synthetic files
better. Small but real (branch `qa/deep-search`).

The bigger lesson was architectural: the experiment exposed that `-q0` configuration leaks
into `-V0` and `-V5`, which is why production high-effort behavior lives behind a dedicated
flag (`--quality-max`) instead of quietly changing existing settings.

## 4. Other landed work

### Podcast constrained optimizer

`tools/podcast` squeezes the best-sounding legal MP3 out of a hard bitrate window. Every
flavor (mode x effort x filter over ABR, CBR, and true-VBR foundations) is landed inside
`[target - 0.5 kbps, target]` by an adaptive search on its rate control, then scored with
the meter, so the comparison is always at nearly equal measured bitrate. The measured
average decides legality, never the requested one.

The headline: true VBR wins big once it can land. On Tom's Diner, lower is better:

| Preset | True VBR | Best ABR/CBR flavor |
| --- | --- | --- |
| stereo 192 (`-V 1.08`, measured 191.70 kbps) | -14.54 | -12.39 |
| mono 96 (`-V 2.5`, measured 95.78 kbps) | -9.26 | -7.71 |

Frame-by-frame bit placement beats average-tracking at equal measured bitrate. Other
receipts: the meter correctly rejects over-eager lowpass (12 and 14 kHz cuts scored +18 dB
on this material) while rewarding gentle ones; `--quality-max` beat `-q4` by ~0.5 dB
in-window. Design and implementation notes: `docs/podcast-optimizer-design.md`.

### Fractional ABR

`--abr 191.5` and `lame_set_VBR_mean_bitrate_float`. An MP3's average bitrate is a mix of
discrete frame sizes, so fractional means were always physically reachable; the
integer-only API was history, not format. Integral values take the legacy integer
arithmetic verbatim (bit-identical); fractional ones refine the per-granule target in
`calc_target_bits` in double precision. Measured: sizes strictly monotone through 191,
191.25, 191.5, 191.75, 192. Gate 70/70.

### CMake, CI, fuzzing, docs lint

The CMake build mirrors the canonical `Makefile.MSVC` Win64 flags exactly and is
**bit-identical to the nmake baseline (70/70)** on MSVC. The gcc/clang path uses plain
`-O2` with strict IEEE semantics; fast-math would silently change encoder output.

CI (`.github/workflows/ci.yml`, live at LynxTWO/lame_v4):

| Job | What it enforces |
| --- | --- |
| windows-bitexact | nmake == CMake parity and `--threads 2` == sequential parity against a runner-built baseline; the committed dev baseline runs as an informational toolchain-drift detector |
| linux-smoke | gcc build; every reference setting encodes and decodes cleanly; meter self-check and a catastrophic-breakage ceiling; docs lint |
| fuzz-decoder | mpglib/hip decoder under libFuzzer and ASan, 60 s per push, seeded by the freshly built encoder |

The first live runs earned their keep: they caught a real 25-year-old portability bug
(config.h defined the stdint type names as macros, fatal against modern glibc) and proved
the meter bit-deterministic across platforms. First fuzz campaign: zero crashes.

`tests/lint-docs.sh` fails CI when tracked Markdown contains typographic Unicode (em
dashes, arrows, checkmarks). Writing rules live in `docs/writing-guide.md`.

### Analysis-front parallelism audit

`docs/analysis-front-audit.md` maps the psymodel/MDCT coupling structure (M/S FFT data is
derived in place by overwriting the L/R work buffers; `MS_thresholds` mixes all four
channels; per-channel state sits in disjoint slots). Verdict: channel-parallel analysis is
bit-exact-feasible but worth only ~1.2x at default settings, so it is deferred; the ABR
frame-batching in Finding 4 was this audit's shipped follow-up.

### Meter hardening

`nmr`'s alignment search now anchors its correlation window at the reference's
highest-energy region (a file's middle can be silence) and only leaves zero offset for a
decisive win. Verified byte-identical results on every real pair tested, so recorded
numbers stay comparable.

## 6. Reviewer decisions

Use an encoder-independent metric. Instrumenting LAME's own objective would be circular: a
search always improves the metric it optimizes. The decoded-domain meter answers the real
question and makes gaming harder. Finding 5 shows what happens even to good internal
metrics.

Keep PEAQ, ViSQOL, and ABX as cross-checks, not the daily driver. Trustworthy PEAQ builds
are Linux-leaning; keeping them off the critical path means tooling risk cannot block
progress. The in-house meter is validated (section 2.2) and sufficient for relative A/B at
equal bitrate. Human ABX remains the final arbiter for anything audible, and for any
default change.

Scope every behavior change. Bug fixes are gated to the exact mode they target and verified
byte-isolated. New behavior is opt-in. Nothing ships default-on without measurement,
byte-isolation, and, when audible, ABX.

Tuning requires train/validation/holdout discipline plus guardrails. Campaigns 2 through 5
are the receipts: mean improvements that memorize the train set or redistribute audibility
look excellent right up until the material is unseen.

## 8. Glossary

**Quantization.** Rounding the audio numbers to fit the bit budget. The rounding is the
lossy part of MP3.

**Masking / masking threshold.** Sounds hide quieter sounds nearby in pitch and time.
Error kept under this threshold is unlikely to be heard.

**Noise-to-mask ratio (NMR).** How far the encoding error pokes above the masking
threshold. Below 0 dB is roughly inaudible. Our `meanNMRdb` is an average; lower is
cleaner.

**CBR / ABR / VBR.** Constant / Average / Variable BitRate. CBR and ABR aim at a fixed
size; VBR aims at a quality level and lets size float.

**`-q0` vs `-V0`.** `-q` is encoder effort (how hard it searches); `-V` is a VBR quality
target. Different dials that interact.

**Bit reservoir.** MP3's trick of saving unused bits from easy moments to spend on hard
ones. Capping frame size with `-B` robs it, which is why the podcast optimizer never does
that.
